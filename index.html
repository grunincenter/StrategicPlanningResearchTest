Tier Criteria

Strong Institutional Commitment*

Schools in this category demonstrate a high level of institutional commitment to social entrepreneurship, impact investing, and/or sustainable development. This commitment is demonstrated through a dedicated, formal center or institute (or a similarly structured program) that acts as a singular organizing hub for the school's relevant offerings. This center coordinates a comprehensive suite of programming, including clinics, courses, student groups, and recurring events/symposia. This structure often involves cross-campus collaboration (e.g., with a business school) and includes the visible support of institutional leadership (e.g., a dean or associate dean).

Substantial Institutional Commitment**

Schools in this category demonstrate a clear focus on social entrepreneurship, impact investing, and/or sustainable development through specific programming, although they may lack the comprehensive institutional commitment of a school with ‚Äústrong institutional commitment.‚Äù Their engagement is typically part of a broader, more general program (e.g., a general entrepreneurship center) and/or lacks a dedicated institutional commitment. This is typically evidenced by a general entrepreneurship clinic that has worked with social enterprises but does not state it as a core mission, or a business law course with a single related component or module. The institutional commitment to these areas is less clear and not a central priority.

Some Institutional Commitment***

Schools in this category offer programming relevant to social entrepreneurship, impact investing, and/or sustainable development, but this is a secondary focus. Their engagement is typically part of a broader, more general program (e.g., programming that is more generally concerned with entrepreneurship, sustainability, community/international development) and lacks explicit keyword focus in the program's current mission or name. This can be seen in a general entrepreneurship clinic that has worked with social enterprises but does not state it as a core mission, or a business law course with a related component. The institutional commitment to this area is less clear.
