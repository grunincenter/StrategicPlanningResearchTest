Research Overview: U.S. Law School Landscape

This dashboard presents research compiled on U.S. Law Schools, focusing on their ranking, tier status, and confirmed/potential summer 2025 programming relevant to specific legal fields. The data combines information on top-tier schools with a broader tiered breakdown.

Key findings suggest a strong concentration of relevant summer programs and high-ranking status among the T14 schools, though significant program activity is also noted across all tiers. This analysis supports strategic planning for student outreach, academic collaborations, and program development.

Process

  • Read the Copy of 2022 Strategic Planning PPT - Law School Research Competitive Landscape and visited the websites cited within.
  • Pulled a list of all 199 accredited law schools, and added them to this Workbook - Accredited law schools tab.
  • Pulled all centers and clinics mentioned in the 2022 PPT and added them to the respective Centers and Clinics tabs, including links and details.
  • Checked websites of all centers and clinics mentioned in 2022, and updated any outdated info on the Centers and Clinics tab.
  • Created keywords to use in searching for any new programming related to our subject areas - see Keywords tab.
  • Created GPT prompts to use in searching school websites - see ChatGPT Prompts tab.
  • Divided all 199 law schools between Nick, Nikki or Zvia, and searched all 199 websites to see if any schools have added programming in our topic areas (using keywords + GPT prompts).
  • Noted programming in grid in Accredited law schools tab, and added details into the following tabs: Centers, Clinics, Courses, Events, Case Studies, CLE, Reports, Extracurriculars.
  • **Note:** For all programming, we saved a PDF of the websites so we would have a record of the offerings, in case websites are later edited.
  • After reviewing all 199 websites, we created an Initial Short List in early August 2025 by looking for "social" within the names of Centers and Clinics
